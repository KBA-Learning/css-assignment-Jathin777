  • Zero To One

  • Peter Thiel

  • "Zero to One: Notes on Startups, or How to Build the Future" by Peter Thiel emphasizes creating groundbreaking innovations rather than merely improving on existing ideas. Thiel argues that true progress comes from developing unique technologies and business models that shift paradigms, rather than incremental changes. The book provides practical advice for entrepreneurs and offers insights into building monopolistic, visionary companies that drive significant advancements.

  • The Lean Startup

  • Eric Ries

  • "The Lean Startup" by Eric Ries introduces a methodology for building startups efficiently by focusing on rapid experimentation, validated learning, and iterative product development. Central to the book is the concept of the Minimum Viable Product (MVP), enabling companies to test ideas with minimal resources. Using the Build-Measure-Learn loop, startups adapt quickly based on real customer feedback, reducing waste and improving chances of success in uncertain environments.
